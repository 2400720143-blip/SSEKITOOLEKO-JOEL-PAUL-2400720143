WHAT IS AFRICAN LITERATURE?

African Literature refers to the body of literary works produced by writers of African descent, both on the continent and in the dispora.It reflects the experiences, traditions and aspirations African people. It includes novels, poetry, drama, and storytellimg in both indigenous and colonial languages.

Some of the prominent African writers include:

  • Chinua Achebe(Nigerian)
  • Ngugi wa Thiong(Kenya)
  • Okot p'Bitek(Uganda)
  • Nadine Gordimer(South Africa)
  • Tsitsi Dangarembga(Zimbabwe)

    • Importance of African Literature.

    1. African Literature including oral traditions like folktales, proverbs, and riddles are crucial for preserving history and diverse cultures.

    2. It broadens one's literary perspective by exposing readers to diverse themes,aesthetics and critical approaches.

    3. Literature written in indigenous african languages helps keep those languages alive and well, which are vital for understanding culture and history.

    4. It allows for the examination of social, religious, economic and environmental challenges through textual analysis.

    5. It helps in restoring and preserving African identity, especially in the face of globalization that can lead to the loss of African heritage.
